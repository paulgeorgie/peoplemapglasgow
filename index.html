An open source geospatial project by GeoGeo.

...because we believe citizens know best.

So we're helping Glaswegians use the latest modern mapping tools
to improve the quality of open data throughout the city.
Meet some of the weapons we'll be using...

OpenStreetMap

Since 2005, OSM has provided citizens with a free and editable map of the world, greatly improving the availability of open geographic data. Sign up today to begin using satellite images to map your neighbourhood!

Quantum GIS

QGIS is one of the leading free and open source geographic information system (GIS) software. We use this to create, edit, visualise, analyse and publish geospatial information. Download your copy now.

Ushahidi

The Kiswahili word for 'testimony', Ushahidi helped Kenyans report who, where, when and what was happening during the 2009 polictic unrest. We aim to develop this platform so you can become a local reporter for Glasgow.

TileMill

TileMill is a design studion that can help you create stunning interactive maps with geographic data. We all have a story to tell, so let us help you to tell it using the latest in stunning web cartography.

State of the Map Glasgow

The map above shows the buildings and roads data, shown as blue polygons and gold lines, respectively. This data is open and freely available via the OpenStreetMap platform and is contributed to by citizens as 'volunteered geographic information'.

...but if you take a closer look, you will notice that this volunteered geographic information does not exist equally. Is it a coincidence that the affluent areas of Glasgow are perfectly mapped and the poorer areas are not? Our mission is to change all that; help us on our mission for Glasgow's geo-justice... click the button below to get mapping using OpenStreetMap; if you live in an unmapped area, why not draw on your neighbourhood today!
